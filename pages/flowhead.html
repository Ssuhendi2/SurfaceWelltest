Flowhead

Comprehensive Flowhead Summary (FOH & POM)

This summary categorizes the Flowhead guidelines into definitions, preparation, installation (rig-up), operations, and maintenance.

1. Definitions, Categories, and Standards

  • Equipment Category: The Flowhead is classified as Category 1.2 equipment. Designed to handle full wellhead pressure (WHP).
  • Barrier Element: The Flowhead is the only Surface Testing equipment considered a Well Barrier Element or Primary Barrier.
  • Design Standards:
    • Must comply with API Specification 6A.
    • For H2S service (WP ≥ 10,000 psi), new Flowheads must be certified to at least PSL-3G.
  • Main Components: Flow Line, Kill Line, and Swivel. (Note: Swivel must be downstream of master valve unless two lubricator valves exist).

2. Preparation and Transportation (Pre-Job)

  • Swivel Check: Ensure swivel is filled with hydraulic oil (not grease) and moves smoothly.
  • Connections: Check compatibility with Well Head Equipment (WHE). Use a saver sub.
  • Torque: Torque all connections before shipment to save rig time.
  • Transportation: Use a basket with secure slings/U-bolts. Support the swivel section to avoid seal damage.

3. Rig-Up Procedures

  • Handling: Use dedicated lifting slings. Support swivel weight during lifting.
  • Kill Line: A Check Valve is MANDATORY between Kill Line hose and Flowhead (installed as close as possible).
  • Marking: Mark all connections with white paint (tally mark) to identify back-off.
  • Function Check:
    • Manual valves: Count turns & write on valve.
    • Hydraulic valves (ESD): Perform stroke test at zero pressure.

4. Pressure Testing

  • Shop Test: Drift test (if requested) and Body Test 1.2 times max well pressure.
  • Wellsite Test:
    • Min: 1.2 times MPWHP.
    • Max: Do not exceed equipment WP.
    • Sequence: 1. Choke/Swab/Master -> 2. Wing/Swab/Master -> 3. Kill line check valve.

5. Operations (Run)

  • Limits: Monitor pressure, temp, tension, and torque.
  • WARNING: Never rotate the Swivel while under pressure.
  • Intervention: Close flow/kill valves before tool passes flowhead. Supervisor attendance required.
  • ESD: Hydraulic actuator holding pressure is approx half of opening pressure.

6. Maintenance

  • Major Recertification (CBM3): Every 5 years.
  • Retirement: Junk before 4th major survey (approx 20 years).
  • Flushing: Flush with fresh water/brine + corrosion inhibitor after jobs.

⚠️ Safety Critical Points

  • Check Valve: Mandatory on Kill Line.
  • Man Riding: Prohibited as primary option.
  • Rotation: Do NOT rotate swivel under pressure.
  • H2S: Must meet NACE MR0175 & PSL-3/3G standards.
