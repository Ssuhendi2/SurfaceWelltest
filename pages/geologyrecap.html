Geology Recap

Comprehensive Summary

This section covers the fundamental geology concepts regarding rock classification, reservoir properties, hydrocarbon traps, and subsurface conditions.

1. Rock Classification

[Image of rock classification chart igneous sedimentary metamorphic]

Rocks are categorized into three groups, but Sedimentary rocks are the primary focus for oil and gas accumulation.

  • Igneous: Solidified magma (e.g., Granite). 95% of earth's crust. Generally non-porous.
  • Sedimentary: Formed from eroded material or precipitates.
    • Clastic: Sandstone, Siltstone, Shale.
    • Non-clastic: Limestone, Dolomite.
    • Note: Oil & Gas are typically found here.
  • Metamorphic: Altered by heat/pressure (e.g., Marble). Generally too non-porous unless fractured.

2. Rock Properties

[Image of porosity and permeability diagram in rocks]

To function as a commercial reservoir, a formation must possess Porosity ($\phi$), Permeability ($k$), and a Trap.

  • Porosity ($\phi$): Percentage of void space.
    • Effective Porosity: Interconnected pore space capable of flow.
    • Dual Porosity System: Contains interconnected secondary porosity (fractures/vugs).
  • Permeability ($k$): The ease of fluid flow (measured in Darcys).
    • Grain Size: Fine grains = Higher friction = Lower permeability.
    • Absolute: 100% saturation of one fluid.
    • Effective: Permeability to a fluid when another phase is present.
    • Relative: Ratio of Effective to Absolute.

3. Hydrocarbon Accumulation (Traps)

[Image of hydrocarbon structural traps anticline fault salt dome]

Hydrocarbons migrate upward until stopped by an impermeable barrier (Trap).

  • Domes & Anticlines: Circular or elongated folding of strata.
  • Salt Domes/Plugs: Salt intrusion piercing overlying strata.
  • Faults: Shearing action places an impermeable bed against a permeable one.
  • Unconformities: New impermeable beds covering older, eroded formations.
  • Lenticular: Pockets or "pinch-outs" (e.g., ancient sand bars).
