Oil Burner

Comprehensive Summary (FOH & POM)

The Oil Burner is critical Category 2.1 surface equipment used for the safe and controlled disposal of produced liquid hydrocarbons during well testing operations.

1. Definition and Classification

  • Classification: Category 2.1 surface equipment (low-pressure section from choke manifold to burner).
  • Primary Function: Safe disposal/combustion of liquid hydrocarbons.
  • Models Mentioned: EverGreen burner, Green Dragon burner.

2. Operational Principles

  • Atomization: Essential for efficient combustion and pollution prevention (fallout). Achieved using compressed air.
  • Pressure Atomization (Green Dragon): Requires a minimum backpressure of **200 psi** upstream of the atomizer.
  • Air Supply: Use of **collapsible hoses is FORBIDDEN**. Hoses must have internal support to prevent airflow restriction.
  • Ignition (Mandatory): A reliable, remotely operated pilot ignition system (typically Propane) is mandatory.
    • Strictly FORBIDDEN to use make-shift ignition lances; ignition must be electrical/pre-installed.

3. Rig-Up and Preparation

  • Mounting: Burners are mounted on booms (e.g., 60ft or 85ft U-booms) to achieve safe distance from the rig.
  • Balancing: A test lift of the complete assembly (burner + boom) is required before load-out.
  • Nozzle Selection: Use **ArchiTest** simulation to confirm correct oil nozzle/burner head sizing.
  • Flushing: **Mandatory** to fully flush oil and air lines before opening the well to remove debris that could plug the nozzles.
  • Valve Testing: Pressure test the burner oil isolation valves against plugged atomizers to check for leaks.

4. Safety and Environmental Protection

  • Heat Management:
    • Mandatory to use a **water shield (water screen)** to protect the rig and equipment.
    • Sprinkler system around the burner and boom must be operational.
  • Wind Direction: Operation must ensure the active burner is **"downwind"** from the rig. Be ready to switch burners if the wind changes.
  • Personnel Safety:
    • A watchman with radio contact must be positioned when personnel are working on the boom.
    • Personnel must **NOT** go out on the boom while burning is in progress.
  • Shutdown Procedure (Pollution Prevention): To prevent hydrocarbon fallout, turn off supplies in this specific sequence: **Propane, Air, then Water.**

5. Maintenance and Lifespan

  • Lifespan: Recommended retirement age is **15 years** (shorter than most other SWT equipment, 20 years).
  • Certification: Requires Major Re-Certification (CBM3) every **5 years** (Visual, thickness checks, hydrostatic tests).
  • Transportation: Burners should generally **NOT** be stacked vertically with booms due to the risk of base plate failure. The burner head must be secured to avoid torsion shocks.

⚠️ Safety Critical Points

  • Ignition: Ignition must be **remote and electrical** (Propane pilot). Manual lances are strictly prohibited.
  • Atomization: Maintain air supply and minimum pressure (200 psi for Green Dragon) to prevent incomplete combustion (fallout).
  • Boom Access: Never access the boom while burning is in progress.
  • Flushing: Always flush lines thoroughly before flow to prevent nozzle plugging.
