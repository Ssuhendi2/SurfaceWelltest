Choke Manifold

Comprehensive Summary (FOH & POM)

The Choke Manifold controls the flow rate and pressure drop of the well effluent. This summary covers the lifecycle including preparation, rig-up, operations, and maintenance.

1. Preparation and Load Out

  • Visual Inspection: Verify manifold is securely fixed to the frame and check for external damage.
  • Component Integrity:
    • Check adjustable choke seat and stem for erosion.
    • CRITICAL: Ensure stem tip is integral to the stem. Screwed or pinned tips are FORBIDDEN.
    • Verify adjustable stem and seat fit correctly and can be set to zero.
  • Protection: Plug all NPT/Autoclave ports. Install a choke bean in each box to protect threads during transport. Transport with valves OPEN.
  • Spares & Accessories:
    • Full set of Choke Beans (8/64" to 64/64" and 72/64" to 128/64") + Blank beans.
    • Tools: Wrenches, valve handles.
    • Parts: Stem packing, seats, gaskets, and gate valve seals.

2. Rig-Up Procedures

  • Placement: Keep high-pressure line from flowhead short. Manifold must be grounded and have a clear escape route.
  • Piping Layout: Min 1 meter straight pipe downstream before any elbows to mitigate erosion.
  • Instrumentation:
    • Install pressure gauges before connecting hammer unions.
    • Sampling: Use two needle valves (Inner = Open, Outer = Functional).
    • Warning: Replace thermowells with blind plugs to avoid washouts.
  • Operational Checks: Cycle all gate valves. Count turns and mark them. Inject grease.
  • Zeroing (Mandatory): Adjust indicator sleeve so "0" aligns when stem contacts seat. Must be done before opening the well.

3. Operations

  • Opening the Well:
    • Start with small adjustable setting (e.g., 8/64" for gas).
    • Open Downstream valves first, then Upstream.
  • Switching Chokes: Use adjustable side for transitions. Flow should be on Fixed Choke for stable periods.
  • Inspection: Visually inspect internal choke box for debris during every bean change.
  • Erosion Monitoring: If solids are present, check wall thickness on elbows/tees/Y-spool every 2 hours.

4. Maintenance & Certification (CBM)

  • Routine (Field): Clean threads, lubricate bearings, inject sealant. Check for erosion.
  • SL1 (Trim): After each job. Visual check, function test, pressure test.
  • SL2 (Annual): Every 12 months. Wall thickness check + Hydrostatic Body Test to WP.
  • SL3 (Major): Every 5 years. NDE + Hydrostatic Test to Test Pressure (1.5x WP).
  • Lifespan: Max service life approx. 20 years.

5. Design Standards

  • Ratings: 5k, 10k, 15k, or 20k psi.
  • Compliance: API Specification 6A and NACE MR0175 (H2S Service).
  • Configuration: Upstream/Downstream valves must have same pressure rating.

⚠️ Safety Critical Points

  • Zeroing: Never flow against a closed (zeroed) adjustable choke.
  • Pinching: Do not operate adjustable choke below 20% opening for extended periods (erosion risk).
  • Downstream Pressure: Ensure pressure does not exceed rating of tanks/separators.
  • Stems: Two-piece stems (screwed/pinned) are strictly prohibited.
