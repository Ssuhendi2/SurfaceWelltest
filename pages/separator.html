Test Separator

Comprehensive Summary (FOH & POM)

The Test Separator is a pressure vessel used to separate well effluent into oil, gas, and water phases for individual metering. This summary covers preparation, rig-up, operations, and maintenance.

1. Preparation and Planning

  • Job Design (ArchiTest): Use ArchiTest software to simulate flow conditions, anticipate erosion, and confirm relief line sizing.
  • Equipment Selection:
    • Relief Valves (PSV): Carry 2 spares. Must have dome spring and inlet shuttle valve/test kit.
    • Seals: Ensure NACE compliance (FKM/PTFE) for H2S/CO2.
    • Metering: Check orifice plates (and spare Teflon carriers). Warning: For HPHT or High Flow (>30 MMscf/d), use non-intrusive thermal wells to prevent washout.
  • Transport: Install side protection bars. Store ball valves OPEN. Remove sensitive instruments (dial gauges).

2. Rig-Up Procedures

[Image of three phase separator internal components diagram]
  • Visual Inspection: Check vessel/frame for cracks. Ensure baffle plates are present (crucial for stabilization).
  • Sight Glasses: Flush if needed. Tighten bolts to 5 m.daN. Verify safety ball valves operate smoothly.
  • Instrumentation:
    • Controllers: Check gas/liquid level controllers. Set regulators to 35 psi.
    • Barton Recorder: Verify pressure integrity and calibration.
    • Thermowells: Remove and inspect for corrosion. Plug ports if flow >30 MMscf/d.
  • Safety Valves (PSV): Install vertically. Discharge piping must be same size as outlet and supported for thrust.

3. Operation

Start-Up & Settings
  • Clean-Up: BYPASS the separator during initial cleanup (mud/solids).
  • Air Tank: Ensure pressure is at least 2 bar.
  • Setting Strategy:
    • High GOR: Low Liquid Level + High Pressure (minimizes gas velocity/carryover).
    • Low GOR: High Liquid Level (maximizes retention time).
Metering & Critical Rules
[Image of orifice plate installation direction]
  • Sequence: Liquid meter first → Gas orifice plate second (once stable). Use 2nd plate size to cross-check.
  • Shrinkage Factor: Measure using shrinkage tester at least twice per choke size to correct oil volume.
  • Shut-In Procedure: DO NOT bypass the separator before shutting in the well. Bypassing causes a sudden pressure drop that ruins downhole gauge data.
  • Erosion: If solids exist, check wall thickness on elbows/tees every 2 hours.

4. Maintenance & Certification

  • SL1 (Every Job): Flush with fresh water, visual check, function test. Test PSVs at 90% set pressure.
  • SL2 (Annual Survey): Ultrasonic wall thickness checks. Remove PSVs (replace with blinds) to pressure test vessel to full Working Pressure (WP). Recalibrate PSVs annually.
  • SL3 (5 Years): Major Recertification. NDE + Hydrostatic test to Test Pressure (1.5x WP). Witnessed by certifying agency.
Pressure Testing Rules
  • Fluids: Use Water/Glycol. Diesel is FORBIDDEN for Category 2 equipment.
  • Gas Testing: Discouraged. If required, must be preceded by Hydrostatic test at 1.2x gas test pressure.

5. Rig Down

  • Draining: Use trapped gas pressure to push fluids to tanks.
  • Flushing: Flush with water/brine + corrosion inhibitor.
  • Transport: Secure inlet/outlet caps. Leave valves OPEN.

⚠️ Safety Critical Points

  • Shut-In: NEVER bypass the separator immediately before a shut-in (affects bottom hole data).
  • Thermowells: Major risk of washout/leakage in high flow wells. Remove or use non-intrusive types.
  • Diesel: Never use diesel for pressure testing separators (Safety Hazard).
  • Bypassing: Always BYPASS during initial well cleanup to prevent sludge blockage.
