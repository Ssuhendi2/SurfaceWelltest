Indirect Heaters & Steam Exchangers

Comprehensive Summary (FOH & POM)

This summary details the classification, rig-up, and operation of Indirect Heaters and Steam Exchangers used to heat well effluent via a medium (water bath or steam).

Note: Direct Heaters (flame contacting flow tube) are not covered in this section.

1. Classification and Standards

  • Category: Category 2 surface equipment (pressure vessels/piping downstream of wellhead).
  • Indirect Type Fired Heaters (IHT): Must comply with API Specification 12K.
  • Steam Exchangers (STX): Must comply with API 660.
  • Location Safety: Must be placed in a Non-Hazardous Area (Safe Area). Offshore use generally requires specific risk assessment and exemption.

2. Types of Heaters

A. Indirect Heater (IHT)

Uses a diesel-fired burner to heat a water bath, which heats the flow coils.

  • Components: Giersch Diesel Fired Burner, Vessel (Tank), Fire Tube (U-Tube), Coil System.
  • Medium: Fresh water or Water/Glycol (50/50 mix) for protection down to -35°C.
  • Power: Requires continuous diesel supply and electrical power (max 45m cable).
B. Steam Exchanger (STX)

Uses steam from an external boiler to heat effluent.

  • Operation: Steam injected into vessel heats the coils.
  • Components: Steam Trap (drains condensate while retaining pressure) and Adjustable Choke (regulates pressure/temp).

3. Preparation and Rig-Up

  • Inspection: Visual check for damage. Routine check by electro-diesel specialist recommended for IHT.
  • Chimney (IHT): Assemble extension on-site. Spark arrester is mandatory.
  • Fluids (IHT): Fill vessel to cover the top of the coil.
  • Purging (IHT): Purge Fire Tube (U-Tube) with compressed air to remove combustible vapors before lighting.
  • Connections: Connect diesel lines, power, grounding, and piping (inlet/outlet).

4. Operations

  • Start-Up (IHT): Begin heating at least 6 hours before opening the well.
    • Start thermostat at 40°C.
    • Operating temp typically max 80°C.
  • Flowing the Well:
    • Cleanup: BYPASS the heater during initial cleanup to prevent mud/solids blockage.
    • Routing Flow: Ensure Outlet Valve is OPEN before opening the inlet to prevent thermal expansion overpressure.
  • Safety Systems:
    • Thermostat: Disconnects power if IHT >80°C (Reset at <70°C).
    • PSV: Ensure Pressure Safety Valves are not isolated.

5. Applications

  • Hydrate Prevention: Keeps gas above hydrate formation temperature.
  • Viscosity Reduction: Improves separation efficiency for heavy oil/emulsions.
  • Foam Prevention: Helps release gas bubbles from oil.

6. Maintenance

  • CBM3 (Major Survey): Every 5 years (Thickness checks, Hydrostatic tests).
  • Pressure Testing:
    • General: 1.5 x Working Pressure.
    • Wellsite: Do not exceed 90% of PSV set pressure if PSVs are installed.

⚠️ Safety Critical Points

  • Area Classification: Strictly Non-Hazardous Area (Safe Zone).
  • Cleanup: Always BYPASS the heater during initial well cleanup (mud/debris risk).
  • Thermal Expansion: Always ensure the DOWNSTREAM (Outlet) valve is open before introducing flow.
  • Purging: Purge the fire tube with air before lighting the burner.
