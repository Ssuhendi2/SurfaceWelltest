Vertical Surge Tank

Comprehensive Summary (FOH & POM)

The Vertical Surge Tank is a pressurized vessel used to verify liquid flowrate measurements, validate meters, and calculate shrinkage factors. Unlike atmospheric gauge tanks, it is mandatory for H2S operations.

1. Definition and Application

  • Design: Pressurized vessel (design pressure ≥ 15 psig).
  • H2S Rule: Surge tanks are MANDATORY for any operation where H2S is expected. (Atmospheric gauge tanks are forbidden in H2S service).
  • Function: Used to verify liquid flow measurements and calculate the "Shrinkage Factor" (volume change from pressure to atmospheric conditions).

2. Preparation and Rig-Up

  • Transportation vs Erection: Transported horizontally but must be erected vertically.
    • Lifting: Use specific top and side lifting eyes to transition from horizontal to vertical. Ensure slings do not damage nozzles/frame.
    • Location: On offshore rigs, must be in a Zone 2 classified area.
  • Connections: Requires 100 psi compressed air, fresh water (flushing), and LP Flare connection.
  • Load Out Checks: Perform ArchiTest calculation to decide if an In-line Adjustable Choke (flow restrictor) is needed at the tank inlet.

3. Operational Guidelines

  • Gas Capacity Verification: Before diverting flow, prove that the vent line can discharge gas without exceeding the vessel's Working Pressure (WP).
  • Conditioning: Establish an initial liquid level with the actual fluid to be measured to avoid interface errors (e.g., water vs oil).
  • Flow Restrictors: Strongly Recommended: Install an adjustable choke upstream of the tank. This protects the tank against "Gas Blowby" from the separator.
  • Measurement: Measure initial/final levels, temp, and volume to calculate the Combined Meter Factor (CMF).

4. Safety and Protection Systems

  • Venting: Vent line must have a Detonation-type Flame Arrestor.
  • Nitrogen Blanketing: Required if the vent line is connected to a flare (to prevent oxygen entry).
  • Cold Venting: Only allowed if NO H2S. A wind sock must be visible to monitor gas cloud direction.
  • Protection Devices:
    • Over-pressure: Relief valves (API 520/521).
    • Vacuum: Breaker valves or Nitrogen blanketing.
  • Grounding: Mandatory to eliminate static electricity.

5. Maintenance & Rig Down

  • CBM3 (Major Recertification): Every 5 years. Includes visual inspection, thickness checks, and Hydrostatic Test.
  • Rig Down:
    • Empty completely of produced fluids.
    • Drain water lines and clean sight glasses.
    • Flushing: Highly encouraged to flush with water/brine + corrosion inhibitor before transport.

⚠️ Safety Critical Points

  • H2S Operations: Use Surge Tank ONLY (Atmospheric Gauge Tank is prohibited).
  • Gas Blowby: Major risk. Use an upstream flow restrictor to maintain backpressure.
  • Lifting: Critical phase. Follow specific procedures to rotate from horizontal to vertical to avoid structural damage.
  • Static: Ensure proper electrical grounding.
