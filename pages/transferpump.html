Transfer Pump (PMP)

Comprehensive Summary (FOH & POM)

The Transfer Pump is designed to pump oil from a gauge/surge tank to a burner or flowline, facilitating continuous operations and pressure boosting for atomization.

1. Definition and Purpose

  • Continuous Operation: Allows emptying one tank compartment while filling another.
  • Pressure Boosting: Provides sufficient pressure for proper atomization at the burner.
  • Fluid Transfer: Transfers water to de-oiling systems, recirculates fluids during testing, or flushes lines during rig down.

2. Safety and Installation

  • MANDATORY Check Valve: A check valve (back pressure valve) must be installed between the oil manifold and the pump.
    • Reason: Pumps are low pressure (usually <300 psi). The valve protects the pump from high downstream pressure.
  • Protection:
    • Non-centrifugal: Must have a pressure relief bypass valve.
    • Centrifugal: Self-protected by operating principle.
  • Location: Safe area or Zone 2 (only if equipped with Explosion-Proof motor).

3. Technology Types

Selected based on fluid characteristics:

  • Gear Type: Compact, cheap spares. Does not accept large solids.
  • Screw Type (Progressive Cavity): Accepts larger solids. Expensive and difficult maintenance.
  • Centrifugal Type: Robust. Highly sensitive to viscosity (inefficient outside range).
  • Diaphragm Type: Safe/cheap for low pressure. Diaphragms degrade with high CO2/Temp.
  • Triplex Type: High pressure/flow. Generates pulses, expensive maintenance.

4. Power Sources

  • Diesel Engine: Autonomous. Requires regular maintenance. generally NOT for hazardous areas.
  • Electrical Motor: Low maintenance. Can be Explosion-Proof.
  • Air Driven: Safe, low maintenance, but limited power.

5. Procurement

  • Local Procurement: Locations with trained supervisors permitted to procure/repair Air-Driven pumps locally.
  • Standard Pumps: Generally shock-protected (DNV 2.7-1 frame) and supplied with full quality files.

⚠️ Safety Critical Points

  • Check Valve: STRICTLY REQUIRED downstream of the pump to prevent back-pressure damage.
  • Pressure Rating: Remember that transfer pumps are Low Pressure equipment (usually max 300 psi).
  • Zone Classification: Do not place Diesel pumps in Zone 2 unless specifically certified.
