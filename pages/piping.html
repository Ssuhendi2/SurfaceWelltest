Pipework (Rigid Piping)

Comprehensive Summary (FOH & POM)

This section covers the preparation, rig-up, operation, and maintenance of rigid piping used in Surface Well Testing.

1. Preparation and Selection

  • Standard Design: All testing pipework is designed for H2S Service (Sour Service). Never mix with standard service piping from other departments.
  • Connection Types:
    • Hammer Unions: Common for standard pressures. Warning: Fig 1502 nut can screw onto Fig 602/1002 but will fail under pressure. Use of 2" Fig 402, 602, and 1002 is forbidden to prevent mismatch.
    • Metal-to-Metal (Grayloc): Mandatory for HPHT (>10,000 psi or >250¬∞F) or high CO2/H2S.

2. Rig-Up and Assembly

  • Handling: Use baskets with separators. Do not drop or pile haphazardly.
  • Layout:
    • Minimize elbows to reduce erosion.
    • Maintain 1 meter straight pipe downstream of the choke manifold.
    • Support piping on wooden blocks (not directly on ground).
  • Flow Direction: Flow enters Female sub → exits Male sub.
  • Assembly: Hand-tighten first to ensure alignment. If it won't hand-tighten, the angle is wrong.
  • Restraints (Mandatory): Use Safety Slings (Polyester Roundslings) on all temporary pipework. Attach using "Half Hitch" or "Choke Hitch" anchored to a fixed object.

3. Operation

  • Walking the Line: Inspect all connections, valves, and restraints before pressure testing.
  • Pressure Testing: Upstream equipment tested to 1.2 x MPWHP.
  • Erosion Monitoring: If solids (sand) are present, check wall thickness on elbows/tees every 2 hours.
  • "Harsh Job" Criteria:
    • Gas > 30 MMscf/d OR Oil > 8,000 bbl/d.
    • Presence of solid particles (sand).
    • High CO2 (>7 psia) or H2S (>0.05 psia).
    • Requirement: Mandatory Pre-job & Post-job thickness checks.

4. Maintenance & Certification

  • SL1 (Service Level 1): Visual inspection after every job.
  • SL2 (Annual Survey): Every 12 months OR immediately after a "Harsh Job" if erosion is detected.
  • SL3 (Major Recertification): Every 5 years. MPI + Hydrostatic Test to Working Pressure.
  • Lifespan: Max 20 years.

üí° Analogy: The "Tire Tread" Concept

Think of pipe maintenance like managing race car tires:

  • Preparation: Choose the right compound (H2S Steel).
  • Rig-Up: Tighten lug nuts correctly (Hammer Unions).
  • Harsh Jobs: High speed (High Flow) or Off-road (Sand) wears the tread down instantly.
  • Maintenance: Just as you measure tire tread depth, you must measure pipe wall thickness before and after a Harsh Job. If the "tread" is too thin, retire the pipe to prevent a blowout.
