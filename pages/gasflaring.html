Gas Flare System

Comprehensive Summary (FOH & POM)

While there is no single hardware section for a "Gas Flare Stack" in the manuals, this summary covers the design, operation, and safety regulations for gas flaring systems routed to burner booms or flare pits.

1. Design and Configuration

  • Sizing: Gas vent lines and flare line connections must be calculated using ArchiTest software based on output flow rate.
  • Booms (Offshore): Gas flare lines are routed alongside oil burner lines.
    • Weight Constraint: Schedule 40 piping with Fig 206 connections is permitted on flare booms (heavier schedules required elsewhere).
  • Land Operations: Typically utilize a Flare Pit for gas disposal.

2. Operational Guidelines

  • Ignition (Mandatory): Must use a reliable, remotely operated pilot ignition system.
    • FORBIDDEN: Using make-shift ignition lances manually.
  • Start-up Sequence: Light the Gas Flare FIRST before directing flow to the Oil Burner.
    • Reason: The gas flare supports the ignition of the oil burner, especially in windy conditions.
  • Wind Management: Flare must be downwind. If wind changes, switch flow to the opposite boom (e.g., Port to Starboard).

3. Connections & Safety Restrictions

  • Atmospheric Tanks (Gauge Tank): DO NOT CONNECT vent lines to the gas flare.
    • Risk: Backpressure exceeding tank design and Oxygen entry into flare system.
  • Pressurized Tanks (Surge Tank): Vent line connects to the relief line/header leading to the LP Flare.
    • Requirement: Must be equipped with a Detonation-type Flame Arrestor.
  • Relief Lines (PSV): Generally not routed to flare unless required by client. If required, injection point must be downstream of the PSV.
  • H2S Operations:
    • Gas bleed ports must be gathered to a vent header/flare (no venting to atmosphere).
    • Weather Rule: If wind drops below 15 knots during H2S flaring, operations may need to cease (unless exempted).

⚠️ Safety Critical Points

  • Atmospheric Tanks: NEVER connect atmospheric tank vents to the main flare line.
  • Ignition: No manual ignition lances allowed.
  • Sequence: Gas Flare supports Oil Burner ignition. Light Gas first.
  • H2S: Monitor wind speed carefully. Low wind (<15 knots) increases gas cloud risk.
